I obtained bachelor's degree in information engineering and media at NTU, Singapore.

Research

Robots planning long-horizon behavior, such as unpacking grocery bags and heating up the takeouts, must be able to plan quickly and execute robustly in semantically rich, geometrically complex environments. I've worked on the following problems in the field of robot learning and planning:
  • Chain imitation learned policies for solving multi-step mobile manipulation problems.
  • Solve long-horizon manipulation problems given any robot embodiment by combining the geometric reasoning ability of TAMP and common-sense knowledge provided by pre-trained vision-language models.
  • Learn to plan for motion while satisfacing geometric collision-free, physical stability, and data-defined spatial constraints (e.g. packing non-convex objects in boxes).
  • Learn to predict task plan feasibility from images of worlds with articulated and movable obstacles.
  • Hierarchical planning and replanning in partially observable Task and Motion Planning problems.
