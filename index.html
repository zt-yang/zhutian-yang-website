Hello! My name is Skye and I'm a PhD candidate at MIT working on robot learning and robot planning for long-horizon mobile manipulation problems. I'm co-advised by Leslie Pack Kaelbling and Tomás Lozano-Pérez in the Learning and Intelligent Systems group. I'm currently a part-time research intern at NVIDIA Seattle Robotics Lab, working with Dieter Fox. I obtained bachelor's degree in information engineering and media at NTU, Singapore.

Research

Household robots planning long-horizon behavior, such as unpacking grocery bags and heating up the takeouts, must be able to plan quickly and execute robustly in semantically rich, geometrically complex environments. I'm working on the following problems in the field of robot learning and planning:
  • Compose diffusion policies and RL-trained policies for multi-step mobile rearrangement problems.
  • Plan with information provided by pre-trained vision-language models.
  • Learn to plan for motion while satisfacing geometric collision-free, physical stability, and data-defined spatial constraints (e.g. packing non-convex objects in boxes).
  • Learn to predict task plan feasibility from images of worlds with articulated and movable obstacles.
  • Hierarchical planning and replanning in partially observable Task and Motion Planning problems.
